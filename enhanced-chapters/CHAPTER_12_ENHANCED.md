# CHAPTER TWELVE
## The Children Who Shouldn't Be

The world came back in fragments.

Cold metal against his wrists. The taste of blood in his mouth. The smell of something acrid and industrial.

And pain.

Aesir's eyes opened to bars. Iron. Close. A cage mounted on something that hummed beneath him. Not the steady vibration of wheels on stone. Something else. A low electromagnetic pulse that made his teeth ache.

He was moving.

---

The cage swayed gently. Hovering. But he could hear the rhythm of hooves ahead. The creak of leather harness. Something pulling the hover platform through uneven terrain.

A cargo pacer. Damascus military design. He'd seen them in books. Hover tech married to draft animals to conserve fuel.

Efficient. Pragmatic.

Prison variant.

His hands were bound behind him. Thick rope, professionally tied. His legs chained to a bolt in the cage floor. The gag was gone. They'd removed it.

They wanted to hear him.

---

"Naska!"

His voice cracked. Raw from screaming he didn't remember.

"Let me out! NASKA!"

One of the guards turned. Damascus uniform. Iron grey with crimson trim. The man's face was scarred. Bored.

"Shut up, boy."

Aesir lunged against the chains. They held.

"Where is she? What did you—"

The guard slammed a baton against the bars. The sound rang through Aesir's skull.

"I said shut up."

---

Aesir tried to reach for it. The power. The cold blue fire that lived beneath his skin.

Nothing.

He focused. Reached deeper. Felt for the hum of electromagnetic current. The potential energy in the metal around him. The kinetic force in the moving pacer.

Nothing.

Just emptiness. A void where his power should be.

Panic clawed up his throat.

"What did you give me!?"

The guards laughed.

"Suppression tonic," the scarred one said. "Damascus specialty. Keeps troublemakers quiet." He spat over the side of the pacer. "You'll get used to the numbness. Or you won't. Doesn't matter."

---

Aesir's breath came fast. Shallow.

The emptiness felt wrong. Like a limb he couldn't feel. A part of himself severed and distant.

He tried again. Focused on the chains. Tried to heat the metal. Tried to feel the frequency of the iron.

Nothing.

"Naska..."

His voice broke.

The last thing he remembered. Her smile. Her lips against his. The warmth of her hand in his.

Then the arrow.

The blood.

Her gasping his name.

---

"Is she alive?"

The guards didn't answer.

"Please. Just tell me if she's—"

The baton slammed the bars again.

"One more word and I'll break your jaw."

Aesir closed his eyes. Forced himself to breathe.

She had to be alive. She had to be.

If she wasn't—

He couldn't finish the thought.

---

Hours passed.

Or maybe minutes. Time felt liquid. Unstable.

The pacer had descended long ago. The air was cool. Damp. The sound of hooves echoed through stone tunnels. Underground.

They were already deep in Uwell.

He'd heard of it. The buried kingdom. Six cities carved into the bones of the earth. A place that survived the War of the Black Queen by hiding beneath the poison.

He'd never imagined he'd see it.

Not like this.

---

The guards relaxed after a while. Aesir had gone silent. Slumped against the bars like he'd given up.

He listened.

There were four of them. Two Damascus soldiers. Two Uwell guards. Mixed detail.

One of the Damascus soldiers—scarred face, grey uniform with crimson trim—shifted in his seat.

"Glad this run's almost over," he said. "Three days underground. I miss the sky."

One of the Uwell guards laughed. "You get used to it. Born down here. Never knew anything else."

"How long you been topside?" the other Uwell guard asked.

The scarred Damascus soldier grunted. "Two years in Karkadann. Then they sent me here. Special assignment."

Aesir's eyes opened. Just a sliver. He didn't move.

"Karkadann," the Uwell guard said. Voice impressed. "The capital. You see Ramstock?"

"Twice a week," the Damascus soldier said. Casual. "He walks the war rooms. Checks progress. Man doesn't waste words."

The second Damascus soldier spoke. Younger. Cleaner uniform. "You hear about the briefing last week? Veskar was there."

The scarred one nodded. "The serpent himself. Overheard some of it. They were talking about this prisoner." He jerked his thumb toward the cage. "Said he was Indexto stock. Wanted him delivered intact."

Silence.

"Indexto?" The Uwell guard's voice dropped. "Thought they were extinct."

"So did everyone," the scarred soldier said. "But Ramstock's intel is solid. If he says the boy's valuable, he's valuable."

---

The younger Damascus soldier laughed. "Your Madam Nidus thinks she's in control. Thinks she brokered an alliance."

One of the Uwell guards bristled. "She did. Damascus needed access to the Orma network. We provided it."

"She's useful," the scarred soldier said. Not unkind. Matter of fact. "For now. Ramstock's patient. He'll let her think she's negotiating. Once we control the trade routes, Uwell's leverage disappears."

The Uwell guard frowned. "The vaults in Ankt are ruins. The real supply flows through our network."

"Exactly," the younger Damascus soldier said. "And once Damascus monopolizes those routes, we expand. Unchallenged."

The Uwell guards went quiet.

The scarred soldier continued. "It's not just about Uwell. Ramstock's already working Utilta. The sealed city. Getting people inside."

"Utilta's capital of Quandrum," the younger soldier added. "Once we have access there, the whole continent opens up."

Silence. Just the rhythm of hooves. The hum of the pacer.

One of the Uwell guards shifted uncomfortably. Said nothing.

---

Aesir's hands curled into fists behind his back.

They'd planned this. All of it.

The abduction wasn't opportunistic. It was surgical.

And Nidus—whoever she was—thought she was making deals with equals.

She had no idea what was coming.

---

The numbness in his chest shifted.

Not fading. Just... changing. A dull throb where the emptiness had been. His thoughts felt sluggish. Heavy. Like wading through mud.

Whatever they'd given him wasn't just suppression. It was something else. Something that made the world tilt and blur at the edges.

His power flickered. Distant. Weak.

Maybe thirty percent. Maybe less.

Aesir kept his breathing steady. His expression empty.

Waited.

---

The pacer slowed. The tunnel widened. He could hear voices ahead. A checkpoint. Uwell guards confirming transit.

The Damascus soldiers called out codes. Papers exchanged.

The draft beast ahead snorted. Tired.

Aesir felt it now. The barest tingle beneath his skin. The electromagnetic current trying to wake through the haze.

Not much. Barely functional.

But he had to try.

He waited until the pacer started moving again. Until the checkpoint was behind them and the tunnel narrowed.

Then he closed his eyes. Focused through the fog.

Felt for the metal of his chains.

---

The iron heated.

Slowly. Barely. Enough to weaken one rivet.

He pulled. Muscles straining.

The chain gave. Not a clean break. A desperate tear.

---

The guards turned.

Aesir moved.

Hands free. Grabbed the bars. Tried to send a pulse through the lock.

The energy sputtered. Weak. Unfocused.

But enough.

The lock sparked. Gave.

The door swung open.

He stumbled out.

---

The scarred guard drew his blade. Aesir caught his wrist.

Too slow. Too clumsy.

The guard twisted free. Blade coming around.

Aesir ducked under it. More luck than skill. Drove his shoulder into the man's chest.

They both went down.

Aesir's fist connected. Once. Twice. The guard's head snapped back.

The second guard swung a baton.

Aesir rolled. The baton hit metal. He kicked out. Caught the guard's knee.

The man stumbled.

Aesir grabbed the cage edge. Pulled himself up. Vision swimming.

The guard came at him again.

Aesir shoved energy into his palm. Barely a spark. Slammed it into the guard's chest.

The man flew back. Hit the tunnel wall. Slid down.

---

The draft beast panicked. Bolted.

The pacer lurched. Aesir lost his footing. Hit the platform hard.

The hover mechanism sputtered. Destabilized.

The pacer tilted. Scraped stone. Sparks everywhere.

Aesir rolled off the edge.

Hit the ground badly. Something in his ribs cracked.

Behind him, the pacer crashed. Metal screaming.

He forced himself up.

Ran.

---

The tunnels twisted.

Aesir ran. Or tried to. His legs felt wrong. Heavy. The world tilted with every step.

He'd never been to Uwell. Didn't know the layout. The roads. The districts.

Stone corridors branched in three directions. Then four. Then more.

A labyrinth.

He chose left. Then right. Then left again.

Lost.

His shoulder throbbed. His ribs screamed. Blood ran down his temple from where he'd hit the ground.

The drug in his system made everything blur. Sounds echoed strangely. Shadows moved when they shouldn't.

He stumbled into a wider road. Underground. Carved from living rock. Glowstone lanterns casting everything in dim orange light.

Empty.

No. Not empty.

Footsteps.

---

Aesir turned.

Four men. Uwell uniforms. City guard.

He tried to raise his hands. Tried to summon power.

Nothing came.

The tall one in front smiled.

"Well. What do we have here?"

Four men. Uwell uniforms. City guard. Patrol.

The one in front was tall. Broad. His face was angular. Sharp. Eyes cold and amused.

"Well," the man said. Voice smooth. Almost friendly. "What do we have here?"

---

Aesir tried to straighten. Raised his hands.

No energy came. Just the faint ghost of what should be there.

"Stay back," he said. Voice slurred.

The tall man—Borum—smiled. "Oh, I don't think so."

He gestured to two of his men. "Take him."

---

The guards moved.

Aesir met the first one.

Tried to block. Too slow. The punch caught his ribs.

He gasped. Swung back. Missed.

The second guard came in low. Swept his legs.

Aesir hit the ground. Hard.

Everything spun.

He tried to push up. A boot caught him in the side. He rolled.

The guards were on him. Professional. Controlled.

He wasn't a threat. Just a half-conscious fugitive.

---

Borum watched. Chuckling.

His men worked efficiently. One held Aesir's arms. The other landed measured strikes.

Aesir tried to fight back. Managed one weak hit. It barely connected.

The guard didn't even flinch.

Aesir's vision darkened at the edges. The drug pulling him under. His body failing.

He couldn't focus. Couldn't think.

Just pain and fog.

---

Borum's smile faded. Boredom settling in.

This wasn't even interesting.

"Enough."

He stepped forward. Casual. Unhurried.

Aesir saw him coming through the haze. Tried to move.

Couldn't.

Borum's fist connected with Aesir's jaw.

Everything went white.

Then black.

---

Aesir hit the ground. Didn't move.

Borum looked down at him. Shook his head.

"That's the infamous Indexto boy? The threat Damascus was so concerned about?"

He nudged Aesir's ribs with his boot. No response.

"Drugged, disoriented, half-dead already." Borum snorted. "Easier than the drunks we pull from the third district taverns."

One of his men laughed.

Borum gestured dismissively. "Bind him. Take him to the main city. Cell block three. Madam Nidus will want to see what all the fuss was about."

The guards moved. Tied Aesir's hands. His feet. Lifted him like cargo.

Borum watched for another moment. Then turned.

"I have more serious matters to attend to."

He walked away.

Left Aesir to the darkness.

To the cell waiting in the buried heart of Uwell.

Where nightmares would find him.

---
