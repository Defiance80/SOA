# CHAPTER FOURTEEN
## Ashes of the Forgotten

Aesir's footsteps echoed through the dimly lit corridors of Uwell's underground labyrinth.

The stone walls were damp and cool. The air thick with the scent of incense and musk.

He felt a sense of unease creeping over him. A feeling that he was being watched. Scrutinized by unseen eyes.

He glanced at Kha'Taar walking beside him. Her expression calm. Almost amused by his discomfort.

"You look nervous," Kha'Taar said. Voice light with a hint of teasing. "Don't worry. My mother doesn't bite... unless she wants to."

Aesir shot her a look. Half annoyed. Half curious. "I've heard a lot about Madam Nidus. Can't say it's put me at ease."

Kha'Taar smirked. Eyes glinting in the low light. "She has that effect on people. But you'll be fine. Just be honest with her. She values honesty above all else."

---

They continued in silence. The corridor opening into a vast hall.

The space was opulent. Filled with rich tapestries and carvings of serpents winding around pillars.

The floor was covered with lush carpets. The air filled with soft laughter and music.

Women and men lounged around. Clothing minimal. Eyes half lidded as they indulged in fruits and drinks. Bodies entwined in pleasures.

The atmosphere was one of decadence. A place where every desire could be satisfied. Every whim indulged.

Aesir couldn't help but be struck by the surreal nature of it all.

Here, deep beneath the earth, far from the sun's light, a world thrived in shadows.

He felt a pang of homesickness. Mind drifting to quiet evenings spent with Raijeen. The warmth of Aelayla's cooking. The familiar sounds of Utilta.

This place, though luxurious, felt alien. Dangerous.

And yet a part of him was drawn to it. To the mystery and allure it represented.

---

At the far end of the hall sat a throne carved from black stone. Entwined with silver serpents that seemed to writhe and move in the flickering torchlight.

On the throne sat Madam Nidus.

Her presence commanding. Regal.

She was a woman of striking beauty. Long dark hair cascading over her shoulders. Skin smooth and flawless.

Her eyes were dark and piercing. Like those of a predator.

Her lips curved in a smile that was both welcoming and dangerous.

Kha'Taar led Aesir forward. Stopping a few feet from the throne.

She bowed her head respectfully. Aesir followed her lead.

Felt the weight of Nidus's gaze upon him.

---

"Mother," Kha'Taar said. Voice respectful but familiar. "This is Aesir. The boy we found. As you requested."

Madam Nidus's gaze moved from her daughter to Aesir. Eyes narrowing slightly as she studied him.

There was a silence. Heavy and expectant. As if she were peeling away the layers of his soul with her eyes alone.

"Thank you, Kha'Taar," Madam Nidus said finally. Voice smooth as silk. "You may go now. I wish to speak with our guest alone."

Kha'Taar nodded and turned to leave. Giving Aesir a quick, reassuring smile before disappearing into the shadows.

Aesir felt a sudden chill. Realizing he was alone with this woman who exuded both power and danger.

---

"Come closer, Aesir," Madam Nidus said. Voice soft but commanding. "Let me see you."

Aesir stepped forward. Movements cautious.

Two muscular guards flanked him. Shirtless. Skin gleaming in the torchlight. Eyes cold. Expressions unreadable.

Nidus gestured. The guards stepped aside.

Allowing Aesir to approach her throne.

He stopped a few feet away. Eyes meeting hers.

Madam Nidus studied him for a long moment. Gaze intense.

Aesir could feel the weight of her scrutiny. As if she were reading the very thoughts in his mind.

Finally she nodded to the guards. "Escort him to the private chamber. I will join him shortly."

---

The guards led Aesir through ornate doors into a smaller, more intimate chamber.

The room was richly decorated. Walls lined with books and scrolls. A plush velvet couch in the center.

The air was warm. The light soft and inviting.

The guards motioned for Aesir to sit. He did so. Mind racing with questions.

Moments later Madam Nidus entered the room. Her presence filling the space.

She closed the door behind her. Walked over to a small table. Poured a glass of wine.

Handed it to Aesir.

He accepted it hesitantly. Unsure of what to expect.

"Relax, Aesir," she said. Voice gentle. "I mean you no harm. I simply wish to talk."

---

Aesir took a sip of the wine. Found it rich and smooth.

Watched as Nidus settled into a chair opposite him. Eyes never leaving his face.

"You are a curious one," Nidus said. Tone thoughtful. "Veskar believes you are something extraordinary. I wanted to see for myself."

She paused.

"Tell me. What do you know of your own abilities?"

Aesir hesitated. "I... manifest. Without enhancement. It shouldn't be possible. Not for a modern Eliant."

"Because you are not a modern Eliant."

The words hung in the air.

Nidus leaned forward. Eyes piercing.

"You are something older. Stranger. The kind of thing that makes powerful men very nervous."

---

She rose. Crossed to a shelf. Retrieved an ancient looking scroll.

Set it on the table between them.

"Do you know of the Indextos, Aesir?"

He shook his head.

"Few do," Nidus said softly. "They were the original inhabitants of Ari Sapeia. Beings of immense power. Masters of what we call spiritual science. They understood the fabric of reality itself. Could manipulate electromagnetic waves. Tap into the spectrum of light. Alter the very vibrations of existence."

She paused.

"Two hundred years before the Eliant Wars, they vanished. Left behind only traces. Ari Sapeia, their homeland, is now protected by a perpetual storm. No one has breached it in centuries."

Aesir felt a chill run through him. "Why are you telling me this?"

---

Nidus's gaze softened. Just a fraction.

"Because I believe you carry their lineage. The readings Veskar took when you manifested in the forest... they match old records. Records I have kept hidden for a very long time."

She leaned back.

"You are here because you are valuable, Aesir. To me, that means protected."

"Protected from what?"

"From people who would use you. Break you. Extract what you are and discard what's left."

Her voice had gone hard.

"I will not be one of them."

---

Aesir searched her face. Trying to read her.

"Why should I trust you?"

Nidus smiled. Small. Almost sad.

"You shouldn't. I am a survivor, Aesir. I have made alliances with dangerous people to keep my city alive. I have done things I am not proud of. But I have never sacrificed my people for power."

She paused.

"I allied with Damascus because I had no choice. Lord Ramstock... he is a tyrant wrapped in etiquette. His right hand, Veskar, is a serpent who believes himself a god. They see Uwell as a resource to exploit. Me as a pawn to control."

Her jaw tightened.

"I let them believe that. Because it keeps them from simply crushing us. But I am not naive. I know what they are. I have warned my daughter. I am looking for a way out from under their thumb."

---

"And I'm part of that?"

"Perhaps." Nidus met his eyes. "Or perhaps you are simply a boy who was taken from his home and deserves better than to be a weapon for men who see people as ammunition."

She rose.

"When the next surface clearing occurs, you may leave. I will not stop you. But if you stay, I will teach you what I know. Help you understand what you are. On one condition."

"What condition?"

"That you stay away from Veskar. He knows you are here. He wants access to you. I have denied him. Will continue to deny him. But if you leave this chamber and wander, he will find ways."

Her voice dropped.

"Do not give him that chance. He will not be as... considerate as I am."

---

Aesir swallowed hard.

Every instinct screamed not to trust her.

But something in her eyes—a weariness, a protectiveness that reminded him, painfully, of Aelayla—made him pause.

"I want to go home," he said quietly. "I want to know if my friends are alive."

Nidus's expression softened further.

"I know. And I will help you learn that, when I can. But for now, you are safer here than anywhere Veskar can reach."

She moved toward the door. Paused.

"Rest. Heal. We will speak again soon. In the meantime, Kha'Taar will see to your needs. Trust her, if you trust no one else."

And then she was gone.

---

## Meanwhile, in Utilta...

The city woke to news that traveled in whispers.

By midmorning, the whispers had reached the palace.

By noon, they had reached Mandu.

Master Eryndor was dead.

---

The body had been discovered by an Academy steward making morning rounds.

Eryndor slumped over his desk. Quill still in hand. As though he'd simply fallen asleep mid sentence.

At first glance, it appeared natural. A man of advancing years. Long hours. The strain of teaching catching up with him.

But the steward had noticed something wrong.

The study, usually meticulous, was subtly disordered. Papers shifted. Drawers not quite closed. Books pulled from shelves and returned at odd angles.

Someone had been there.

Someone who knew how to make a search look like nothing at all.

---

Mandu arrived within the hour.

Bandar at his shoulder. Both men in the stiff, controlled silence that came before bad news confirmed itself.

The Academy Headmaster met them at the door. An old man himself. Face grey.

"General Dakshad. General Sulph. I... I'm sorry to summon you for this. But given recent events, I thought—"

"Show me," Mandu said.

---

The study was exactly as the steward had described.

Eryndor at his desk. Face peaceful. No obvious wound. No struggle.

Mandu's eyes swept the room.

Saw the small things.

A drawer left half open. Dust patterns on the shelf indicating removed volumes. The inkwell placed slightly off center, as though someone had moved it and put it back without quite remembering where it belonged.

"Has anyone touched anything?"

"No, sir. We sealed the room the moment we realized."

Mandu nodded. Crossed to the desk.

Eryndor's last entry was dated two days prior.

The day before the survey left for the forest.

The entry itself was brief:

*Subject continues to display unprecedented baseline manifestation. Request follow up consultation with Gen. Dakshad re: his eldest son.*

---

Mandu felt something cold settle in his chest.

"Bandar. Check the shelves. Anything on Eliant physiology. Enhancement theory. Manifestation studies."

Bandar moved methodically through the room.

Returned five minutes later. Face grim.

"Three volumes missing. All advanced theory. And his personal research journal."

Mandu closed his eyes.

"He wanted to speak with me. After the training yard. After he saw Aesir manifest without limiters."

His voice was very quiet.

"I told him we'd meet when I returned from the border. I never made time."

---

Bandar's hand came down on his shoulder. Heavy. Solid.

"This was not your fault."

"Wasn't it?" Mandu opened his eyes. Looked at his oldest friend. "He saw something. Wanted to warn me. I was too busy. Too proud. Too convinced I had time."

He turned back to the desk.

"Someone knew he was studying Aesir. Someone knew what he'd found. They silenced him and took his research."

"The timing," Bandar said slowly. "Aesir taken. Eryndor killed. Within days of each other."

"Within hours," Mandu corrected. Voice flat. "This was coordinated. Professional. Someone cleaning up loose ends."

---

He crossed to the bookshelf. Ran his fingers along the spines.

Found what he was looking for wedged behind a row of older volumes. A single folded page. Overlooked in the search.

Unfolded it.

Read.

His jaw tightened.

Handed it to Bandar without a word.

---

Bandar read aloud. Voice low.

*"Baseline readings impossible for modern Eliant physiology. No enhancement signature. No limiter bypass. Power source appears endogenous—originating from subject's own biology, not external Eltech augmentation.*

*Manifestation pattern matches archival descriptions from pre-Utiltan records. Specifically: Indexto documented abilities, circa Second Age.*

*Hypothesis: Subject may carry dormant genetic markers from extinct lineage. Recommend immediate consultation. If confirmed, implications are—"*

The entry ended there.

Torn off mid thought.

---

"Indexto," Bandar breathed. "The old myths. The first people."

"Not myths," Mandu said quietly. "Eryndor believed they were real. Believed Aesir carried their blood."

He took the page back. Folded it carefully. Slipped it inside his coat.

"We tell no one about this. Not the council. Not the palace watch. No one."

"Mandu—"

"Whoever did this has reach, Bandar. Reach inside Utilta. Inside the Academy. Maybe inside the palace itself."

His voice dropped.

"They're already inside our walls. And they're hunting anything connected to my son."

---

Bandar was quiet a long moment.

Then: "What do we do?"

Mandu looked at Eryndor's body. At the peaceful face of a man who'd died trying to help.

Who'd been killed for it.

"We find who gave the order," he said. "We trace the thread back to its source. And we prepare."

"For what?"

"For the possibility that the war didn't end when Aesir was taken. It just moved inside our gates."

---

**✦✦✦**

In the high library, behind a door that was more often shut than not these days, Raijeen sat surrounded by books.

Old books. Forbidden books. The kind Aelayla had quietly made available to him without ever saying why.

He'd been reading for days. Since the news came. Since his brother was declared missing.

Since the world had proven, once again, that it would take everything from him if he let it.

He looked up when Aelayla entered. Carrying tea she knew he wouldn't drink but brought anyway.

"You should rest," she said softly.

"I'll rest when I find it."

"Find what?"

Raijeen gestured to the scattered volumes. "Whatever they're afraid of. Whatever Aesir is that made them take him instead of kill him."

---

Aelayla set the tea down. Sat beside him.

Said nothing for a long moment.

Then: "Your father believes Eryndor was murdered. That someone is erasing anyone who knows too much about what you and your brother are."

Raijeen's hands stilled on the page.

"Then they'll come for you next. You know more than anyone."

"Perhaps." Aelayla's voice was calm. "But I am not afraid. I have been keeping this secret for seventeen years. I will keep it until my last breath if I must."

She paused.

"But you, Raijeen. You cannot study your way to safety. Knowledge will not protect you from blades in the dark."

"Knowledge," Raijeen said quietly, "is the only thing that has ever protected me."

---

He turned a page.

"They took Aesir because he manifested in front of Veskar. Because his body showed them something they've been looking for."

His silver eyes lifted. Met hers.

"I won't make the same mistake. I'll learn to hide it. Control it. Until I'm strong enough to come for him."

Aelayla's hand covered his.

"You are already strong, my love. Do not confuse strength with vengeance."

"I'm not," Raijeen said.

His voice had gone somewhere she hadn't heard before.

Somewhere cold and decided.

"I'm confusing it with being smart enough not to lose anyone else."

---

**✦✦✦**

Far beneath the earth, in a warm chamber in Uwell, Aesir sat alone.

Thinking about Nidus's words.

About Indextos and lineages and the shape of the trap he'd walked into.

Kha'Taar had brought food. He'd barely touched it.

She'd left him a small oil lamp. Said her mother wanted him comfortable.

Wanted him safe.

From Veskar. From Damascus. From the very people Nidus had allied with to keep her city alive.

It made a strange kind of sense.

And no sense at all.

---

He lay back on the couch.

Closed his eyes.

Saw Naska's face in the dark. The arrow punching through her neck. The way she'd looked at him like she was asking a question he didn't know how to answer.

Saw Eryndor, a man he'd barely known, wanting to speak with Mandu about something important.

A conversation that would never happen now.

Saw the threads tightening.

Two cities. Two conspiracies. One boy caught between them.

And somewhere in the dark, men in clean clothes making ledgers of lives and deciding which ones still served a purpose.

---

He opened his eyes.

Stared at the stone ceiling.

Made a decision.

If Nidus would teach him to understand what he was, he would learn.

Not because he trusted her.

But because knowledge, right now, was the only weapon he had.

And when the time came—when he was strong enough, smart enough, ready enough—he would walk out of this city.

He would find Naska.

He would go home.

And God help anyone who stood in his way.
