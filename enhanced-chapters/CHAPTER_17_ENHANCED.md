# CHAPTER SEVENTEEN
## The Silent War

Back in City Two, Aesir and Kha'Taar didn't waste time.

They had to confront Madam Nidus.

She'd led them into this. Now they needed answers.

As they entered her grand chamber, the tension between them was palpable.

Kha'Taar's face was a mix of anger and sorrow as she approached her mother.

"Mother," she said. Voice low and filled with emotion. "We need to talk."

---

Madam Nidus sat on her throne. Expression unreadable.

She gestured for them to come closer. "What is it, my dear?"

Kha'Taar hesitated. Eyes glinting with frustration. "Veskar. He told us everything. You've been working with him, haven't you?"

Madam Nidus sighed. Elegant features softening. "I made deals, yes. But it was for the greater good. For Uwell."

"For Uwell?" Kha'Taar spat. Anger boiling over. "You've put us all in danger. Veskar is dangerous. He's growing more powerful every day."

---

Madam Nidus's eyes flickered with a sadness Aesir hadn't seen before.

"I know, Kha'Taar. But I didn't see another way. I had to protect my people. My legacy. The Zorda..."

She trailed off.

For the first time, Aesir saw the vulnerability in her.

This wasn't just about power.

It was about survival. About family.

"I got in over my head," she admitted. Voice barely a whisper. "But it was never malicious. I wanted to save us. I wanted to save you."

---

Aesir watched the exchange. Mind racing.

Madam Nidus was dangerous, yes. But she was also human.

She'd made mistakes. Now they were all paying the price.

Kha'Taar's face softened. Anger giving way to something deeper. "I love you, Mother. But I don't know if I can trust you anymore."

Madam Nidus smiled sadly. "In Uwell, my dear, even family must sleep with one eye open."

---

Aesir stood silently as the tension between Kha'Taar and her mother hung in the air like a heavy cloud.

He hadn't seen Kha'Taar like this before. Caught between love and loyalty to her mother, and the stark reality of betrayal.

He'd learned to read her over the months they'd spent together. Now her eyes burned with a mixture of hurt and anger that even her stoic warrior demeanor couldn't hide.

Nidus's throne room was as opulent as the rest of City Two.

The silver serpentine designs that curled around her chair shimmered in the dim light. An echo of the powerful woman who ruled from that seat.

Despite the elegance of her surroundings, there was something cold about the space.

Something lonely.

Aesir had seen similar things in the ruins of Utilta. Powerful people with empty hearts.

---

Madam Nidus motioned for Kha'Taar and Aesir to sit on the velvet couch near her throne.

Kha'Taar, still seething, chose to remain standing. Hand never leaving the hilt of her dagger.

Aesir followed her lead. Watching the way the muscles in her jaw tightened every time her mother spoke.

"I know Veskar is dangerous," Madam Nidus said softly. Voice filled with rare vulnerability. "But you have to understand, Kha'Taar—there are forces beyond us at play. You've seen what Uwell has become. We are an underground city. Living on the remnants of a land devastated by war."

She paused.

"The Zorda, my dear, was supposed to be our salvation. I thought if I worked with Lord Ramstock and Damascus, we could bring prosperity to Uwell again."

---

Kha'Taar crossed her arms. Eyes blazing. "You think handing over control of Uwell to Veskar is salvation? He was going to kill us. He's not an ally—he's a monster. You don't see it because you're blinded by your deal with Damascus."

Nidus's face tightened. "I see it, Kha'Taar. I see it every day. But the Zorda is more than a relic. It's power—true power. I thought I could control Veskar. But it's become clear I've miscalculated."

---

Aesir's heart pounded as he listened.

The Zorda. That was the key to all of this.

He'd heard whispers about it. The ancient tablets that supposedly unlocked hidden abilities and knowledge.

But he hadn't realized how deep the conspiracy went.

Ramstock. Damascus. Veskar.

All of them after this ancient power.

"You're playing with fire, Nidus," Aesir said. Voice colder than he'd intended. "Veskar isn't someone you can manipulate. He's got his own plans. I don't think they align with yours."

---

Madam Nidus's gaze shifted to Aesir. Eyes studying him intently.

For a moment, the usual sharpness in her expression softened.

"I know you want to leave, Aesir. I see it in your eyes every time you walk through these halls. But you've become part of something much bigger than any of us. The Indextos, the Eliants, the Zorda... they are all connected. And whether you believe it or not, you are tied to this power."

Aesir's breath caught in his throat.

The word Indextos still rang in his ears like a distant echo.

He'd never heard it until recently. It still didn't sit right with him.

Everything he knew, everything he'd been told, was unraveling before his eyes.

---

"I've heard the stories about the Indextos," Aesir said slowly. "But they're just myths. No one's even set foot on Ari Sapeia in centuries. And what does any of this have to do with Veskar?"

Nidus's expression darkened. "Veskar... he's after more than just power in Uwell. He wants something that goes beyond this city. Beyond the Orma trade. The Zorda is part of that. And if he gains control of it—if he unlocks its secrets—he will be unstoppable."

The silence in the room was deafening.

Aesir's mind raced as he tried to piece together the puzzle.

He knew there was more to his past. More to his connection with Uwell and the Eliants than he understood.

But this?

This was something far greater.

---

"So what do we do?" Kha'Taar asked. Voice quiet now. Anger replaced by something like fear.

Nidus rose from her throne. Crossed to a window overlooking the dark streets of City Two.

"We prepare," she said. "Veskar believes he has the upper hand. That I'm nothing more than a pawn in Damascus's game. But I have kept one card close. One he doesn't know about."

She turned back. Eyes sharp again.

"The Zorda's location. He's been searching for it for years. Believes I'll hand it over to secure my position. But I never will. Not to him. Not to Ramstock."

---

"Then why tell him you had it at all?" Aesir asked.

Nidus smiled. Bitter. Tired.

"Because in negotiations with predators, you need bait. I gave him hope. Hope that kept him from simply crushing Uwell outright. But hope is a double edged sword. It makes men patient. And patient men grow complacent."

She paused.

"I've been buying time. Waiting for the moment when I could strike. When I could turn Damascus's own weapon against them."

Kha'Taar's eyes widened. "You've been planning to betray them."

"I've been planning to survive," Nidus corrected. "There's a difference."

---

She crossed back to them. Took Kha'Taar's hands in her own.

"I know I've put you in danger. Both of you. And I'm sorry. But everything I've done—every alliance, every compromise—has been to keep this city alive long enough to find a way out."

She looked at Aesir.

"And then you arrived. A boy with power Damascus covets. Power Veskar would kill to study. But also power that could tip the balance."

Aesir felt his stomach drop. "You want to use me. Against them."

"I want to give you a choice," Nidus said. "Stay. Help me protect Uwell from what's coming. Or leave. When the next surface clearing happens, I'll get you out. No strings. No debts."

She paused.

"But know this. If you leave, Veskar will hunt you. Damascus will hunt you. They know what you are now. They won't stop until they have you."

---

The weight of her words settled over Aesir like a shroud.

He'd spent months wanting to leave. To get home. To find Naska. To see his family.

But home felt further away than ever.

And the boy who'd left Utilta—the one who'd believed in walls and safety and clean answers—was gone.

Replaced by someone harder. Someone who'd killed raiders in the Cursed Lands and felt the thrill of it.

Someone who didn't know anymore if he'd recognize the life he'd left behind.

---

"I need time," Aesir said finally. "To think."

Nidus nodded. "Of course. But not too much time. Things are moving faster than I'd like. Veskar won't wait forever."

She gestured to the door. "Go. Both of you. Rest. We'll speak again soon."

As they left the chamber, Kha'Taar's hand found his again.

"I'm sorry," she said quietly. "For all of this. For my mother. For dragging you deeper into this mess."

Aesir squeezed her hand. "You didn't drag me anywhere. I walked in myself."

---

They walked in silence back to Aesir's quarters.

At the door, Kha'Taar hesitated.

"Can I... stay? Tonight? I don't want to be alone."

Aesir nodded. Opened the door.

Inside, she sat on the edge of his bed. Shoulders slumped. The weight of the day finally catching up with her.

Aesir sat beside her. Close but not touching.

"Do you believe her?" Kha'Taar asked. "That she's trying to protect us?"

"I think she believes it," Aesir said. "Whether it's true or not... I don't know."

---

Kha'Taar leaned against him. Head on his shoulder.

"I used to think my mother was invincible. That she could outthink anyone. Outplay anyone. But now..."

Her voice broke.

"Now I see she's just as lost as the rest of us."

Aesir put his arm around her. Held her as she cried.

It was the first time he'd seen her break.

The first time the warrior fell away and left just a girl who was scared and alone and didn't know who to trust anymore.

---

When the tears finally stopped, she looked up at him.

Eyes red but determined.

"Whatever you decide—stay or go—I'm with you."

"Kha'Taar—"

"I mean it," she said. "My mother chose her path. I'm choosing mine. And I choose you."

Before Aesir could respond, she kissed him.

Not the stolen kisses in alleyways. Not the hurried touches in the dark.

This was different.

Desperate. Real. A promise wrapped in fear and hope and the weight of everything they'd lost.

---

When they finally pulled apart, Aesir rested his forehead against hers.

"I don't know what I'm doing," he admitted. "I don't know if I'm making the right choices. I don't even know who I am anymore."

"Then we'll figure it out together," Kha'Taar said. "One step at a time."

She curled against him. And for the first time in months, Aesir felt something like peace.

It wouldn't last. He knew that.

The storm was coming. Veskar. Damascus. The Zorda. All of it converging on Uwell like a wave about to break.

But for now, in this moment, he wasn't alone.

And that was enough.

## Meanwhile, in Utilta...

Raijeen stood in the high library. Surrounded by books he shouldn't have access to.

Books Aelayla had quietly made available without ever saying why.

His silver eyes scanned the pages. Taking in information faster than most people could read.

He'd always been like this. Quick. Absorbent. Able to see patterns others missed.

But since Aesir's disappearance, that ability had become something else.

An obsession.

---

He was looking for answers. For clues. For anything that might explain what his brother was and where he'd been taken.

And slowly, piece by piece, he was finding them.

The old texts spoke of beings called Indextos. Masters of spiritual science. Wielders of power that transcended normal human limits.

They'd vanished two hundred years before the Eliant Wars.

Left behind only fragments. Ruins. Stories.

But Raijeen was beginning to suspect the stories were more than myth.

---

The door opened. Aelayla entered with tea he wouldn't drink.

"You should rest," she said softly.

"I'll rest when I find it," Raijeen replied. Not looking up.

"Find what?"

"Whatever they're afraid of. Whatever Aesir is that made them take him instead of kill him."

---

Aelayla sat beside him. Quiet for a long moment.

Then: "Your father believes Eryndor was murdered. That someone is erasing anyone who knows too much about what you and your brother are."

Raijeen's hands stilled on the page.

"Then they'll come for you next. You know more than anyone."

"Perhaps," Aelayla said calmly. "But I am not afraid. I've been keeping this secret for seventeen years. I'll keep it until my last breath if I must."

She paused.

"But you, Raijeen. You cannot study your way to safety. Knowledge won't protect you from blades in the dark."

---

"Knowledge," Raijeen said quietly, "is the only thing that has ever protected me."

He turned a page.

"They took Aesir because he manifested in front of Veskar. Because his body showed them something they've been looking for."

His silver eyes lifted. Met hers.

"I won't make the same mistake. I'll learn to hide it. Control it. Until I'm strong enough to come for him."

Aelayla's hand covered his.

"You are already strong, my love. Do not confuse strength with vengeance."

"I'm not," Raijeen said.

His voice had gone somewhere she hadn't heard before.

Somewhere cold and decided.

"I'm confusing it with being smart enough not to lose anyone else."

---

The city outside slept. Certain of its walls.

Unaware that in a high library, a thirteen year old boy with silver eyes and blue hair was becoming something dangerous.

Unaware that far beneath the earth in Uwell, his brother was making choices that would change the shape of the world.

Unaware that the threads connecting them—blood, power, destiny—were pulling tighter with every passing day.

And when those threads finally drew them back together, the world would learn what it meant to take something from the sons of a line older than empires.
