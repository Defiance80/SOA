## FULL SCENE: MARIK DRAVEN'S HUMILIATION (Chapter 9)

### **PART 1: The Comedy**

The lower end of the Ninth smelled like everything Utilta pretended didn't exist.

Smoke. Sweat. The particular funk of too many people living in too little space with not enough water.

Aelayla walked through it like she belonged. Which, Aesir was learning, she did. More than the Academy ever showed.

Askan walked on her left. Naska on her right. Aesir trailing slightly behind, watching the way she moved through the crowd. Easy. Unbothered. A noblewoman in silk walking through a place that ate nobles and didn't leave bones.

"Economics," Aelayla was saying, "is the lifeblood of a society. Money flows like blood through a body. When circulation fails—when it doesn't reach all the parts—the body suffers. Rots."

She gestured at the streets around them.

"Poverty isn't a moral failing. It's failed circulation. The heart pumps, but the blood pools at the center. The extremities die."

Naska frowned. "So fix the circulation."

"Easier said than done. The people at the heart don't feel the rot. They're warm. Fed. Why would they change the system when it's working for them?"

"Because the body dies if the extremities fall off," Askan said.

"Eventually. But 'eventually' is a long time when you're comfortable now."

Aesir opened his mouth to ask a question—

And the brothel door ahead of them exploded open.

---

A man came stumbling out.

Tall. Well-built. No shirt.

**No pants.**

Just underclothes. And not enough of those.

He spun. Panicked. Looked back at the door.

"MY TROUSERS! SOMEONE STOLE MY—"

Two figures bolted past him. Teenage boys. One waving a pair of fine trousers like a banner. Laughing.

The man—Aesir recognized him now, Marik Draven, one of the faces from the deeper fights—froze.

Not because of the theft.

Because of the crowd.

---

A dozen people had stopped to watch.

Silence.

Then someone laughed.

Then everyone laughed.

"I didn't know they accept newborn size!" a dockworker called out.

The crowd roared.

"He must be terribly cold!" an old woman cackled.

"Hey, little fella!" A man cupped his hands around his mouth. "Your mother know you're out here without a jacket?"

More laughter. Hoots. Jeers.

Draven's face went from red to purple.

---

A woman stepped out of the brothel. Arms crossed. Face like stone.

She had the trousers. The boys had circled back, tossed them to her.

She threw them at Draven's feet.

"Next time," she said, voice carrying, "pay your existing tab before you try to run a new one."

The crowd lost it. Absolutely howled.

Draven snatched up the trousers. Struggled into them. Hands shaking with rage.

Someone slow-clapped.

---

Aelayla's lips twitched. She bit down on a laugh. Failed.

A small, decidedly unladylike snort escaped.

She covered it. Poorly.

Naska was openly grinning. Askan had turned away, shoulders shaking.

Aesir tried to keep his face straight. Couldn't.

Aelayla caught his eye. Shook her head.

"Come," she said, ushering them forward. Past the spectacle. "We've seen enough."

As they walked away, her voice was low. Amused. But underneath it, something sadder.

"Every city has its folly of fools. Even this one."

---

Behind them, Marik Draven finished dressing. Pushed through the jeering crowd.

Did not look back.

But Aesir saw his face as he passed.

Saw the rage. Cold. Controlled. Not the hot embarrassment of a man caught out.

The ice of a man making a list.

---

### **PART 2: The Horror (Two Days Later)**

Two days later, they were in the same district when they smelled the smoke.

All four of them. Walking back from the training yards.

Aelayla stopped. Turned her head.

"Fire."

The crowd was already running. Toward it. The way crowds do when disaster strikes somewhere that isn't their house.

Naska grabbed Aesir's arm. "That's—"

She didn't finish.

They ran.

---

By the time they got there, the building was gone.

Just smoke. Ash. The skeletal frame of what had been walls.

The Ninth's volunteer brigade stood around it with empty buckets. Too late. Had been too late before they'd even arrived.

The doors—what was left of them—were scorched black.

Someone had barred them from the outside.

Aesir could see where the wood had been reinforced. Nailed. From the wrong side.

"No one got out," a man said. Voice hollow.

Aelayla's face was stone.

Naska's hand was still on Aesir's arm. Tight enough to hurt.

Askan stared at the ruin. Silent.

---

"How many?" Aelayla asked. Voice very quiet.

"Six confirmed. Maybe seven. They're still—" The man gestured vaguely at the smoking wreckage. Couldn't finish.

"The owner?"

"Inside."

"The girls?"

"All of them."

Silence.

---

Someone in the crowd muttered a name.

Just loud enough.

"Draven."

Others picked it up. Whispered. Passed it along like a curse.

No one said it loud. No one accused. But everyone knew.

The Ninth always knew.

---

Aelayla turned away. Jaw tight.

"Come."

Her voice left no room for argument.

They followed.

---

When they were a street away, out of the smoke, Aelayla stopped.

Looked at the three of them.

Her face was tired. Older than it had been an hour ago.

"Remember this," she said. "Not the fire. The doors."

"What?" Aesir asked.

"The doors were barred from outside. That's not an accident. That's not a candle knocked over. That's murder."

Naska's voice was flat. "Draven."

"Can't prove it. Won't ever be able to prove it. But yes."

Aelayla's hands curled into fists. Released.

"Two days ago, we laughed. He was a fool. Humiliated. Harmless."

She gestured back toward the smoke.

"Seven people paid for that laugh. And it wasn't even their joke."

---

Askan spoke for the first time. Voice very young.

"What do we do?"

"Nothing." Aelayla's voice was bitter. "There's nothing to do. He's too connected. Too protected. The city won't touch him. The Ninth can't."

"So he just... wins?"

"He already won. The moment he decided the price of his pride was worth seven lives and no one could stop him."

She looked at each of them in turn.

"That's the lesson. Not economics. Not circulation. This."

She pointed at the smoke rising behind them.

"Power isn't what you can build. It's what you can burn and walk away from."

---

Naska's voice was quiet. Shaking.

"The house always wins."

"Yes."

"And the house is built on bodies."

Aelayla closed her eyes. Nodded.

"Yes."

---

They walked home in silence.

Behind them, the Ninth buried its dead and added another name to the long list of things it knew but could never prove.

And Marik Draven, in his clean room in his guarded house, slept soundly.

Because he could.
